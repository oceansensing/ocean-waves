Deep Water Waves

kh = 4.0  |  h/λ = 0.637  |  h > λ/2
Orbit: Circular ↻   Decay: ekz (exponential)   Phase speed: c ≈ √(g/k)   Group speed: cg = c/2

Intermediate Water Waves

kh = 1.2  |  h/λ = 0.191  |  λ/20 < h < λ/2
Orbit: Elliptical   Decay: cosh/sinh kernel   Phase speed: c = √(g tanh(kh)/k)   Group speed: c/2 < cg < c

Shallow Water Waves

kh = 0.25  |  h/λ = 0.040  |  h < λ/20
Orbit: Flat ellipse (strongly elongated)   Horizontal: Depth-uniform   Phase speed: c ≈ √(gh)   Group speed: cg ≈ c (non-dispersive)

Wave Regime Classification Criteria

Deep Water
h/λ criterion h/λ > ½
kh criterion kh > π ≈ 3.14
tanh(kh) ≈ 1  (error < 0.4%)
Orbit decay ekz — negligible at z = −λ/2
Examples Ocean swell (λ ≈ 150 m) in water > 75 m depth; wind waves in deep ocean; seismic T-waves.
Intermediate Water
h/λ criterion 1/20 < h/λ < ½
kh criterion 0.31 < kh < 3.14
tanh(kh) Full dispersion relation required
Orbit shape Elliptical; eccentricity increases with depth
Examples Swell shoaling onto the continental shelf; coastal long-period waves; harbor resonance.
Shallow Water
h/λ criterion h/λ < 1/20
kh criterion kh < π/10 ≈ 0.31
tanh(kh) ≈ kh  (error < 2%)
Orbit shape Strongly flattened ellipse; horiz. nearly depth-uniform
Examples Tsunamis in ocean basins (λ ~ 200 km, h ~ 4 km); tides; storm surges in coastal zones.
Note on h/λ vs kh The two criteria are equivalent: kh = 2πh/λ, so h/λ = kh/(2π). The tanh(kh) approximations are accurate to <1% error at the stated boundaries. Practical rule of thumb: swell with T = 10 s has λ ≈ 156 m in deep water (c = gT/2π); it enters shallow-water regime in h < 7.8 m, and deep-water regime applies for h > 78 m.
